A Hindu is someone who practices Hinduism, a religion with many different gods and goddesses. The term "Hindu" can also refer to someone who has a cultural connection to Hinduism. How do people become Hindus? People are often born into Hindu families and practice the faith. People can also declare themselves to be Hindu. There is no formal conversion process into or out of Hinduism. What do Hindus believe? Hindus believe that the universe is God's, and that everything in the universe is God. Hindus believe that each person is divine, and that the purpose of life is to realize that divinity. Hindus believe that no one religion is exclusively true, and that all major faiths are aspects of a universal truth. Hindus value ahimsa, or nonviolence. What are some Hindu practices? Vegetarianism: Many Hindus practice vegetarianism, which means they do not eat meat. Pilgrimage: Hindus visit holy sites, rivers, and mountains. Festivals: Hindus celebrate festivals such as Diwali and Holi. Yoga: Some Hindus practice yoga to gain control over their minds and become closer to the divine.
